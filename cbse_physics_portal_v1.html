Physics All Around You

Class XII · Modern Physics
The Atom
Electrons orbit a dense nucleus — this tiny structure is the basis of all matter, chemistry, and nuclear energy.
Powers MRI scanners, nuclear reactors, LED lights
Class X & XII · Optics
Light & Prism Dispersion
White light splits into its 7 colours when it passes through a prism — VIBGYOR. This is also why rainbows form.
Rainbows, camera lenses, optical fibre, spectroscopy
Class XI · Oscillations
Simple Pendulum & SHM
A swinging pendulum converts potential energy to kinetic energy and back — a perfect example of Simple Harmonic Motion.
Grandfather clocks, seismometers, suspension bridges
Class XII · EM Waves
Electromagnetic Waves
Electric and magnetic fields oscillate perpendicular to each other — creating light, radio waves, X-rays, all at 3×10⁸ m/s.
WiFi, mobile phones, X-rays, microwave ovens, TV signals
V = IR · P = I²R
Class X & XII · Electricity
Electric Circuits
Current flowing through resistance creates voltage — Ohm's Law is the foundation of every electronic device you use.
Every phone, laptop, home appliance runs on this
Class XI · Kinematics
Projectile Motion
A ball thrown at an angle follows a parabolic path — gravity pulls it down while horizontal velocity carries it forward.
Cricket shots, basketball, rockets, satellites

Teaching Methodology
How We Teach Physics

Evidence-based pedagogical approaches aligned with NEP 2020 — moving from rote learning to conceptual mastery, inquiry-based exploration, and real-world application.

Our methodology follows the 5E Model: Engage → Explore → Explain → Elaborate → Evaluate — proven by research to deepen physics understanding.
🎯
Conceptual First Approach
Every topic begins with the "why" before the "what". Students understand the physical intuition before encountering formulas.
  • Open with a real-world phenomenon (lightning, bridges, MRI)
  • Build intuition through discussion & questioning
  • Introduce mathematical model only after understanding
  • Derive formulas rather than present them
🔬
Inquiry-Based Learning
Students are scientists, not spectators. Each concept is anchored in a guided discovery experiment before formal instruction.
  • Pose a testable question each class
  • Students predict outcomes before observing
  • Discuss anomalies — the heart of science
  • Formalize observations into principles
📐
Socratic Questioning
Teacher-led dialogue that builds reasoning chains. Students learn to think in physics, not just recall physics.
  • Never tell students what they can discover
  • Use Bloom's Taxonomy question ladder
  • Peer-to-peer explanation (protégé effect)
  • Error analysis: "why were you wrong?"
🖥️
Technology-Enhanced Learning
Digital simulations and animations make invisible physics visible — from electric fields to quantum behavior.
  • PhET simulations (University of Colorado)
  • NCERT e-content & Diksha platform modules
  • YouTube derivations & 3Blue1Brown style visuals
  • Virtual lab for concepts hard to replicate
🗺️
Mind Mapping & Concept Linking
Physics is one interconnected web. Students build visual knowledge maps connecting all chapters, classes, and concepts.
  • Weekly 20-min "connect the dots" session
  • Cross-chapter application problems
  • Build a Physics Glossary Notebook
  • End-of-year mega mind map project
🏆
Spaced Repetition & Retrieval
Forgetting is the enemy of mastery. Systematic review schedules ensure concepts are retained long-term.
  • Daily 5-min "flashback" from previous topic
  • Weekly cumulative 15-question quiz
  • Monthly comprehensive revision test
  • Pre-board full syllabus mock sequence

Lab Safety & Notebook Protocol
Lab Record Best Practices
📋
Lab Notebook Format
  • Aim, Apparatus, Theory, Diagram (pre-lab)
  • Observation table — handwritten, in pen
  • Calculation & Result with units
  • Precautions & Sources of error
  • Teacher signature per experiment
🦺
Safety Protocol
  • Lab induction mandatory before first experiment
  • No food or water in lab at any time
  • Electrical experiments — teacher approval required
  • Report any breakage to lab assistant immediately
  • Fire extinguisher and first aid kit location known to all
📊
Viva Preparation
  • Know the principle behind every experiment
  • Memorize formula and units for each measurement
  • Practice explaining precautions in your own words
  • Calculate percentage error for your result
  • Mock viva in class the week before board practical

Growth Tracking
Progress measured relative to the student's own baseline — not just class rank. Every improvement celebrated.
  • Monthly growth chart updated per student
  • Conceptual mastery score per chapter
  • Error pattern log — recurring mistakes flagged
  • Positive reinforcement for effort, not just results
🚨
Early Warning System
Students at risk are identified early — before it becomes a crisis. Three-tier intervention system.
  • Yellow Alert: below 50% in 2 consecutive quizzes
  • Orange Alert: below 33% in a unit test
  • Red Alert: parent contact + remedial plan
  • Peer tutoring program for yellow-alert students
🎯
Personalised Intervention
Each student who is struggling gets a customised recovery plan — not generic "study harder" advice.
  • 1-on-1 teacher session to diagnose root cause
  • Topic-specific extra practice sheet
  • Concept video recommendation
  • Re-test opportunity after intervention period
